# Audience Framing  (loads on slides & resources)

Audience: **non-technical methodological researchers** — fluent in chat/ChatGPT, new to
IDEs/terminals/git/agentic AI. Every slide and handout must obey:

**Do**
- Frame agentic AI as **delegation, supervision, and reproducibility** — not a "faster chatbot."
- Lead with their **values**: rigor, transparency, human judgment, integrity.
- Map onto schemas they already own: **RA supervision, pre-analysis plans, peer review, lab notebook.**
- Show **failure modes + the guardrail** that catches them. Honesty earns this audience; magic loses it.
- Use the **same word for the same concept** everywhere (see `resources/glossary.md`).
- Introduce **one new idea at a time**; keep slides to ≤ ~6 bullets.

**Don't**
- Hype / inevitability framing ("adapt or be left behind") — reads as a sales pitch.
- Jargon-first (ReAct, MCP, orchestration) before the mental model lands.
- A flawless demo with no failures, or hand-waving integrity/hallucination concerns.
- Show a terminal, raw JSON, or config files in the *first* exposure — pure cognitive load.
